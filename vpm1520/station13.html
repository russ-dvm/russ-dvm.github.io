Fresh spleen

Focus your attention on the yellow-tan plaques on the capsular surface.

Histological image of the spleen

Consider the colours present in this image, and try to think of how they may have been generated.

Question 1: Describe the lesion.

Multifocally along the capsular surface of the spleen, there are pinpoint to 1-2 mm plaques that are white to tan.

Question 2: What is responsible for the change in colour?

I apologize for this question; I thought I covered this material in my lecture but I do not. These are examples of what are known as "siderotic plaques" (also known as Gamna-Gandy bodies or siderofibrotic plaques). They are accumulations of pigment laden macrophages - often a mixture of hemosiderin, bilirubin, and other pigments. They are incidental lesions that are commonly found at necropsy in older dogs. This will not be on the exam.
