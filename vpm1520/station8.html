Question 1: Describe the lesion.

Within the mesenteric fat surrounding the pancreas there are multifocal to coalescing, variably sized (up to 1 cm), areas that are white, firm, and slightly gritty.

Question 2: Provide a morphological diagnosis.

Severe, (acute OR subacute), multifocal to coalescing necrosis of fat.

Question 3: What is the pathogenesis?

Acute necrotizing pancreatitis (commonly called "pancreatitis") --> leakage of pancreatic enzymes (e.g. lipase) --> digestion of adjacent fat --> necrosis, +/- mineralization.
